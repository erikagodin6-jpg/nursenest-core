A 68-year-old client is admitted to the cardiac step-down unit with an acute heart failure exacerbation. Assessment: HR 104, BP 158/92, RR 22, SpO₂ 92% on room air, bilateral crackles to mid-lung fields, 2+ pitting edema, and JVD. Which action should the nurse implement FIRST?

Why B is Correct
SpO₂ 92% indicates hypoxemia requiring immediate oxygenation. High-Fowler's positioning reduces venous return to the heart, decreasing preload and relieving pulmonary congestion. This addresses airway and breathing (ABCs priority) before pharmacological intervention. Furosemide, while appropriate, follows stabilizing oxygenation.
