AApply O₂ at prescribed rate and reassess SpO₂ in 15 minutes
BIncrease O₂ to 4L to quickly correct hypoxemia

Management of Care Priority Question

The nurse is caring for four clients. Which client does the nurse assess first?

Client A has a closed femur fracture, pain 6/10, neurovascular checks intact.
Client B had an appendectomy 2 hr ago, SpO₂ 96%, pain controlled.
Client C has Type 2 diabetes, blood glucose 14 mmol/L, and reports thirst.
Client D has a new onset of confusion and slurred speech following a head injury.

AClient A — femur fracture, pain 6/10
BClient B — 2 hr post-appendectomy
CClient C — T2DM, glucose 14 mmol/L
DClient D — new confusion + slurred speech after head injury ✓
Correct — here's why:New neurological changes (confusion + slurred speech) after head injury suggest increased intracranial pressure — an immediately life-threatening deterioration. NCLEX priority = airway, breathing, circulation, then neuro changes. Client D is assessed first.

Sample · Psychosocial Integrity

A nurse is caring for a client who recently received a diagnosis of terminal cancer. The client states, "I know I'm going to die. I just want to go home." Which response by the nurse is most therapeutic?

A"I understand. Let me speak with the team about discharge planning."
B"It's normal to feel that way. Have you talked to your family?"
C"It sounds like being at home is very important to you. Tell me more about what that would mean."
D"Many patients feel this way. Would you like to speak to a counsellor?"
Rationale: Option C uses reflection and open-ended questioning — the most therapeutic communication technique. It acknowledges the client's wish without deflecting or rushing to problem-solve.

Pharmacology · High-yield

A nurse is preparing to administer IV vancomycin 1 g over 60 min. During infusion, the client develops facial flushing, pruritus, and hypotension. Which intervention is the nurse's priority?

AStop the infusion and notify the prescriber
BSlow the infusion rate and reassess in 10 minutes
CAdminister diphenhydramine IV as prescribed
DDiscontinue vancomycin permanently
Rationale: These are signs of Red Man Syndrome — an infusion rate-related reaction, not a true allergy. The intervention is to slow (not stop) the infusion. Stopping entirely is reserved for true anaphylaxis.
