Medication safety rationale

Hold digoxin and assess before administering.

The symptoms suggest possible digoxin toxicity. A novice-safe nursing action is to stop the medication from reaching the patient until assessment and escalation clarify risk.

Transferable principle: assess for toxicity and hold high-risk medications when concerning cues appear.

Why not the others?

Food may reduce nausea but does not address toxicity. Teaching that halos are expected is unsafe. Antiemetics treat a symptom while missing the medication risk.

Key concept

Medication questions often test whether the nurse recognizes when routine administration is no longer safe.
