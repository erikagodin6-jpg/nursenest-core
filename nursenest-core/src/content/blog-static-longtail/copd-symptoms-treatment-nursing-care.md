<h2>Clinical overview</h2>
<p>Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease combines chronic bronchitis and emphysema patterns with fixed or poorly reversible airflow obstruction. Nursing exams emphasize exacerbations, gas exchange, medication adherence, and energy conservation.</p>

<h2>Symptoms and progression</h2>
<p>Progressive dyspnea, chronic cough, sputum production, barrel chest in advanced disease, accessory muscle use, and fatigue. Exacerbation stems add increased sputum, fever, or wheeze changes.</p>

<h2>Treatment themes</h2>
<p>Smoking cessation, combination inhaler therapy, pulmonary rehabilitation, vaccinations, oral steroids and antibiotics during exacerbations per orders, and noninvasive ventilation when indicated. Exam answers align with GOLD-style teaching without requiring memorized staging numbers.</p>

<h2>Oxygen therapy safety</h2>
<p>Long-term oxygen when chronic hypoxemia criteria are met in teaching; titrate to ordered targets; watch for CO2 retention signs when high flows are used in susceptible patients; never discontinue oxygen abruptly if dependent.</p>

<h2>Nursing care priorities</h2>
<p>Pursed-lip breathing coaching, pacing ADLs, nutrition support, infection surveillance, medication teaching with spacer technique, and early escalation when exacerbation criteria appear.</p>

<h2>NCLEX-style reasoning pattern</h2>
<p>Stabilize life threats, trend objective data, match interventions to orders and protocols, teach prevention, and escalate when red flags exceed your independent nursing scope.</p>

<h2>Key Takeaways</h2>
<ul>

  <li>Exacerbation items bundle infection, bronchospasm, and work of breathing.</li>

  <li>Oxygen decisions must match orders and target saturations, not guesswork.</li>

  <li>Patient teaching reduces readmissions in exam narratives.</li>

</ul>

<h2>FAQ Schema Questions</h2>

<h3>Why is smoking cessation still a priority if damage is chronic?</h3>
<p>It slows progression and reduces exacerbation risk—common correct teaching answer.</p>

<h3>What is pursed-lip breathing for?</h3>
<p>Prolongs expiration, reduces dynamic hyperinflation sensation, and helps dyspnea in COPD teaching models.</p>

<h3>What vital trend should raise concern during oxygen titration?</h3>
<p>Rising PaCO2 or somnolence in a CO2 retainer context—escalate per orders and monitoring.</p>
