<h2>Exam tips for OT students</h2>
<ul>
<li>Start by naming the occupation at risk, not only the impairment label.</li>
<li>Prefer answers that include measurable observation, education, or environmental change over vague encouragement.</li>
<li>When disciplines overlap, choose language that reflects OT’s unique lens on participation without overstepping medical decisions.</li>
<li>If a stem includes new red-flag symptoms, prioritize escalation and safety before routine teaching.</li>
<li>Select assessments that match the stated referral question and setting constraints.</li>
<li>Avoid answer choices that promise independent medication or imaging decisions as a student or as OT outside scope.</li>
</ul>
