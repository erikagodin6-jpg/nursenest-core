<h2>Respiratory Therapy Practice Questions</h2>
<p>Respiratory therapy exams test physiology, oxygenation, ventilation, clinical judgment, and critical-care concepts. Practice questions help students apply respiratory concepts instead of relying only on memorization.</p>

<h2>Question 1</h2>
<p><strong>ABG:</strong> pH 7.30, PaCO2 55 mmHg, HCO3 25 mEq/L.</p>
<p>Which interpretation is most accurate?</p>
<ul>
<li>A. Respiratory acidosis</li>
<li>B. Respiratory alkalosis</li>
<li>C. Metabolic acidosis</li>
<li>D. Metabolic alkalosis</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Correct answer:</strong> A.</p>
<p><strong>Rationale:</strong> The patient is acidotic with elevated carbon dioxide, indicating respiratory acidosis from hypoventilation.</p>

<h2>Question 2</h2>
<p>A ventilated patient suddenly develops a high-pressure alarm. Which problem should the RT suspect first?</p>
<ul>
<li>A. Circuit disconnection</li>
<li>B. Bronchospasm or airway obstruction</li>
<li>C. Improved compliance</li>
<li>D. Low oxygen flow</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Correct answer:</strong> B.</p>
<p><strong>Rationale:</strong> High-pressure alarms commonly indicate increased airway resistance such as bronchospasm, secretions, or obstruction.</p>

<h2>Question 3</h2>
<p>A COPD patient has increasing drowsiness and rising PaCO2 levels. Which finding is most concerning?</p>
<ul>
<li>A. Mild cough</li>
<li>B. Hypercapnia with declining mental status</li>
<li>C. Slight tachycardia</li>
<li>D. Dry mouth</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Correct answer:</strong> B.</p>
<p><strong>Rationale:</strong> Rising CO2 with worsening mental status may indicate respiratory failure and requires urgent assessment.</p>

<h2>Question 4</h2>
<p>Which oxygen delivery device can provide high concentrations of oxygen to critically ill patients?</p>
<ul>
<li>A. Nasal cannula</li>
<li>B. Venturi mask</li>
<li>C. Non-rebreather mask</li>
<li>D. Simple face mask</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Correct answer:</strong> C.</p>
<p><strong>Rationale:</strong> Non-rebreather masks can deliver high oxygen concentrations when functioning correctly with an inflated reservoir bag.</p>

<h2>Question 5</h2>
<p>A patient with severe asthma develops wheezing, tachypnea, and increasing work of breathing. What should the RT recognize?</p>
<ul>
<li>A. Stable respiratory status</li>
<li>B. Potential respiratory deterioration</li>
<li>C. Expected mild anxiety only</li>
<li>D. Improved airway function</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Correct answer:</strong> B.</p>
<p><strong>Rationale:</strong> Increasing respiratory distress may indicate worsening airflow obstruction and possible respiratory failure.</p>

<h2>Why Practice Questions Matter</h2>
<p>Practice questions strengthen clinical reasoning, pattern recognition, and exam endurance. RT students should review rationales carefully and identify why incorrect answers are less appropriate.</p>

<h2>Key Takeaway</h2>
<p>Respiratory therapy exam success depends on understanding oxygenation, ventilation, ABGs, airway management, and patient stability. Consistent question practice improves confidence and clinical reasoning.</p>
