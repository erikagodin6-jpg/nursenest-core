<h2>Clinical overview</h2>
<p>Pulmonary embolism obstructs pulmonary vessels, impairing gas exchange and sometimes causing hemodynamic collapse. Items reward recognition, oxygenation support, and preparing for ordered anticoagulation or advanced therapies.</p>

<h2>Signs and symptoms</h2>
<p>Sudden dyspnea, pleuritic chest pain, tachycardia, cough, hemoptysis, syncope, and hypoxemia out of proportion to lung sounds. Massive PE stems may show hypotension or shock.</p>

<h2>Risk factors</h2>
<p>Immobility, surgery, malignancy, estrogen therapy, pregnancy, prior VTE, and thrombophilia appear repeatedly. Connect risk to prevention teaching.</p>

<h2>Nursing priorities</h2>
<p>ABC stabilization, high-flow oxygen as needed, IV access, continuous monitoring, preparing for ECG and labs, bleeding precautions once anticoagulation starts, and emotional support during uncertain diagnostics.</p>

<h2>Exam traps</h2>
<p>Do not anchor only on calf pain—PE can present with isolated dyspnea. Avoid walking a suspected unstable patient to tests alone.</p>

<h2>NCLEX-style reasoning pattern</h2>
<p>Stabilize life threats, trend objective data, match interventions to orders and protocols, teach prevention, and escalate when red flags exceed your independent nursing scope.</p>

<h2>Key Takeaways</h2>
<ul>

  <li>Hypoxia plus tachycardia plus risk factors should trigger PE suspicion in exam vignettes.</li>

  <li>Anticoagulation requires renal function and bleeding assessment baselines.</li>

  <li>Massive PE pathways are provider-driven; nursing focuses on stabilization and monitoring.</li>

</ul>

<h2>FAQ Schema Questions</h2>

<h3>What is the most common symptom of PE?</h3>
<p>Dyspnea is frequent, but presentations vary—use the full cluster.</p>

<h3>Why is anticoagulation used?</h3>
<p>It prevents clot propagation and recurrence when not contraindicated in teaching frames.</p>

<h3>What should you monitor after starting heparin?</h3>
<p>Bleeding signs, platelets for HIT surveillance per protocol, aPTT or anti-Xa per order set, and renal function.</p>
