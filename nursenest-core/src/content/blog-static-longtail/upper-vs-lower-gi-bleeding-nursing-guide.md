<h2>Clinical overview</h2>
<p>Gastrointestinal bleeding threatens perfusion through volume loss. Nursing exams reward rapid stabilization, source clues, and safe preparation for endoscopy or surgery pathways.</p>

<h2>Upper GI bleeding clues</h2>
<p>Hematemesis, coffee-ground emesis, melena, epigastric pain, and hypotension with variceal or ulcer sources in teaching vignettes.</p>

<h2>Lower GI bleeding clues</h2>
<p>Bright red blood per rectum, maroon stools, bleeding diverticula, ischemic colitis, or hemorrhoids when the stem supports benign anorectal sources.</p>

<h2>Shared priorities</h2>
<p>Two large-bore IVs, type and screen, fluid resuscitation per orders, monitor hemoglobin trends, NPO, orthostatic vitals, Foley for accurate output in shock teaching, prepare for transfusion.</p>

<h2>Medication awareness</h2>
<p>PPI infusions for ulcer bleeds when ordered, octreotide and antibiotic themes in variceal contexts per protocol, avoid NSAIDs, review anticoagulation plans with provider.</p>

<h2>NCLEX-style reasoning pattern</h2>
<p>Stabilize life threats, trend objective data, match interventions to orders and protocols, teach prevention, and escalate when red flags exceed your independent nursing scope.</p>

<h2>Key Takeaways</h2>
<ul>

  <li>Melena suggests upper tract slow bleeding in classic teaching; brisk upper bleeds can present with hematochezia when rapid transit occurs.</li>

  <li>Hemodynamic instability always outranks endoscopy scheduling in priority questions.</li>

  <li>Airway protection matters when ongoing hematemesis threatens aspiration.</li>

</ul>

<h2>FAQ Schema Questions</h2>

<h3>What is the first priority in massive GI bleed?</h3>
<p>Airway if vomiting blood, then circulation support with IV access and ordered fluids or blood.</p>

<h3>Why is orthostatic hypotension significant?</h3>
<p>Suggests meaningful intravascular volume depletion in many frames.</p>

<h3>When is NPO important?</h3>
<p>Before procedures such as endoscopy when ordered and until risk of aspiration is clarified.</p>
