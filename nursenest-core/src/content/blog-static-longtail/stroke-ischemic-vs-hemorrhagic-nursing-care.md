<h2>Clinical overview</h2>
<p>Stroke is acute focal brain injury from ischemia or hemorrhage. Exams reward rapid recognition, airway and swallow safety, blood pressure nuance, and avoiding interventions contraindicated in hemorrhage.</p>

<h2>Ischemic stroke</h2>
<p>Thrombotic or embolic occlusion; sudden focal weakness, speech deficits, visual field cuts, ataxia. Time-last-known-well drives reperfusion eligibility in teaching items.</p>

<h2>Hemorrhagic stroke</h2>
<p>Often thunderclap headache, vomiting, rapid decline, very elevated BP, and decreased consciousness. Anticoagulation reversal and neurosurgical themes appear when intracerebral or subarachnoid contexts fit.</p>

<h2>Shared nursing priorities</h2>
<p>Airway, oxygenation, glucose check, NIHSS-style neuro monitoring when provided, seizure precautions if ordered, strict BP parameters per etiology, NPO until swallow screen, fall and aspiration precautions.</p>

<h2>Exam contrast points</h2>
<p>tPA and thrombectomy are ischemic pathways with exclusion criteria; lowering BP too aggressively in ischemia without guidance can worsen penumbra—follow stem orders.</p>

<h2>NCLEX-style reasoning pattern</h2>
<p>Stabilize life threats, trend objective data, match interventions to orders and protocols, teach prevention, and escalate when red flags exceed your independent nursing scope.</p>

<h2>Key Takeaways</h2>
<ul>

  <li>CT non-contrast is the classic first imaging branch in many vignettes to rule out bleed before thrombolysis.</li>

  <li>Swallow screening protects from aspiration pneumonia.</li>

  <li>Never give anticoagulants or tPA when hemorrhage is suspected without imaging clearance in exam logic.</li>

</ul>

<h2>FAQ Schema Questions</h2>

<h3>What does FAST stand for?</h3>
<p>Face droop, Arm weakness, Speech difficulty, Time to call for help—mnemonic for public and learner recognition.</p>

<h3>Why is blood pressure management different between ischemic and hemorrhagic stroke?</h3>
<p>Bleeding expansion risk dominates hemorrhagic frames; perfusion penumbra concerns appear in ischemic frames until reperfused.</p>

<h3>What is a priority nutrition intervention before oral intake?</h3>
<p>Swallow assessment per protocol to prevent aspiration.</p>
