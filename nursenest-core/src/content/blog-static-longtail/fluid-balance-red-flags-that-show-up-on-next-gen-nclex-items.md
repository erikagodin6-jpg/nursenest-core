<h2>Trends beat snapshots</h2>
<p>Exam items frequently embed a change over time: weight up overnight, intake exceeding output, new basilar crackles, or restlessness with tachypnea. The correct answer often depends on whether you notice the <strong>direction of change</strong>, not whether a single value looks “borderline.”</p>

<h2>Build a quick scan checklist</h2>
<ul>
  <li>Compare current vitals and work of breathing to the patient’s baseline implied by the stem.</li>
  <li>Link intake routes (oral, IV, medications in fluid) to total fluid load when provided.</li>
  <li>Ask what the earliest nursing action is that reduces harm while you gather the next data point.</li>
</ul>

<p>Use this checklist across lessons on fluid and electrolytes, cardiac monitoring, and renal injury so the habit transfers to unrelated stems.</p>
