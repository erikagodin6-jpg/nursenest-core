# NurseNest Outcomes Reporting Framework

Date: 2026-06-01

Status: Ethical outcomes framework. No unsupported pass-rate claims should be published.

## Purpose

Define how NurseNest can report learner success ethically, defensibly, and in a way that supports trust without overstating causality.

## Current State

No repository evidence reviewed in this audit demonstrated a mature public pass-rate reporting system. That is safer than unsupported claims. Competitors often publish pass-rate or pass-predictor claims with footnotes; NurseNest should only do this after a defensible methodology exists.

## Allowed Outcome Categories

| Category | Example | Public use |
|---|---|---|
| Platform activity | Questions answered, study sessions completed, simulations completed | Allowed if aggregated and anonymized. |
| Learning progress | Readiness score improvement, weak-area improvement | Allowed with methodology and limitations. |
| Self-reported exam outcomes | "Learners who responded to a survey reported..." | Allowed only with sample size, eligibility, survey method, time window, and bias disclosure. |
| Verified exam outcomes | Institution-verified or document-verified pass outcomes | Allowed only with consent and strict privacy controls. |
| Institutional outcomes | Cohort completion, competency progress | Allowed in aggregate with school/hospital approval. |

## Prohibited Claims Without Evidence

- "Guaranteed pass" unless legally reviewed and backed by clear terms.
- "X% pass rate" without sample size, inclusion criteria, survey timing, response rate, and confidence interval.
- "Most accurate predictor" without validation study.
- "Official NCLEX CAT simulation" unless officially licensed/validated.
- "Clinically proven" without clinical education study.

## Survey Methodology

Every survey report must disclose:

- Survey dates.
- Population invited.
- Number invited.
- Number responded.
- Response rate.
- Inclusion/exclusion criteria.
- First-time vs repeat test-taker split.
- Country/exam split.
- Whether results are self-reported or verified.
- Whether respondents received incentives.
- Confidence interval.
- Limitations and survivorship/response bias.

## Readiness Predictor Methodology

If NurseNest reports readiness prediction:

- Publish what data inputs are used.
- Separate educational readiness from pass prediction.
- Validate against known outcomes before public predictor claims.
- Report calibration error where available.
- Avoid claiming equivalence to NCSBN CAT scoring.

## Public Page Requirements

Any outcomes page must include:

- Methodology section.
- Limitations section.
- Last updated date.
- Data privacy statement.
- No individualized medical, legal, employment, licensure, or exam guarantee.
- Link to editorial/content review policies.

## Compliance Considerations

- Obtain explicit consent for exam-outcome use.
- Do not expose protected learner records.
- Avoid school/hospital naming without written approval.
- Keep advertising claims aligned with actual measured population.
- Keep refund/pass-guarantee claims separate from educational outcome reporting.

## Launch Gate

No outcome statistic may appear in marketing, pricing, comparison, email, or schema until:

- Methodology is documented.
- Legal/compliance review completed.
- Data source audited.
- Sample size and confidence interval approved.
- Public limitations included near the claim.
