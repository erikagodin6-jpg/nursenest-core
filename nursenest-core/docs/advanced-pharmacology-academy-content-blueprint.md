## Product Positioning

Advanced Pharmacology Academy should support medication-class mastery, clinical monitoring, adverse-effect recognition, safe administration, patient education, and advanced practice prescribing readiness where appropriate. It must remain scope-aware for RN, PN/RPN, NP, allied health, and future institutional use.

## Medication Class Template

Every medication class should include:

| Section | Required Content |
| --- | --- |
| Mechanism | What the medication does and why it works |
| Indications | Common reasons for use |
| Contraindications | Absolute and major cautions |
| Monitoring | Labs, vital signs, symptoms, toxicity indicators |
| Patient education | Practical safety teaching |
| Clinical pearls | High-yield bedside or prescribing insight |
| Prescribing considerations | NP or advanced-practice scope when applicable |
| Administration safety | RN/PN/RPN administration and monitoring considerations |
| Interactions | High-risk medication and condition interactions |
| Related content | Conditions, labs, ECG effects, skills, questions, flashcards |

## Readiness Domains

| Domain | Signals |
| --- | --- |
| Medication-Class Recognition | Identifies class, mechanism, and purpose |
| Contraindication Safety | Recognizes unsafe use and high-risk conditions |
| Monitoring Readiness | Links medication to labs, ECG, vitals, and symptoms |
| Patient Education | Provides accurate and practical teaching |
| Adverse-Effect Recognition | Detects common and severe reactions |
| Scope-Aware Practice | Separates administration, monitoring, and prescribing responsibilities |

## Global Reuse Strategy

Target reusable global content: 70-85%.

Medication mechanisms and safety principles are reusable, but names, availability, prescribing rules, formularies, and scope expectations may require country and profession supplements.
