## NCNZ Competency Mapping

Every content item should map to one or more:

1. Professional responsibility.
2. Management of nursing care.
3. Interpersonal relationships.
4. Interprofessional healthcare and quality improvement.

Competency mapping should be stored as metadata for:

- Lessons.
- Questions.
- Flashcards.
- Simulations.
- Practice Exams.
- CAT Exams.

## Te Tiriti o Waitangi Requirements

This is a core curriculum area, not an optional specialty.

Dedicated coverage must include:

- Partnership.
- Participation.
- Protection.
- Equity.
- Maori health outcomes.
- Health system obligations.
- Culturally safe care.

Te Tiriti principles should also be integrated across all clinical content.

## Cultural Safety Requirements

Dedicated content should include:

- Reflective practice.
- Bias awareness.
- Power dynamics.
- Person-centred care.
- Equity-focused care.
- Indigenous health perspectives.
- Communication considerations.

Cultural safety should appear throughout the curriculum rather than existing in a single isolated module.

## Community and Primary Care Requirements

Dedicated coverage must include:

- General practice nursing.
- Public health nursing.
- Chronic disease management.
- Health promotion.
- Community assessment.
- Rural healthcare.
- Home care.

## Medication Governance Requirements

Must include:

- Safe administration.
- Documentation.
- Monitoring.
- Medication incidents.
- High-risk medications.
- Controlled medication practices.

## Clinical Assessment Requirements

Must include:

- Deterioration recognition.
- Escalation.
- Vital signs interpretation.
- Clinical judgment.
- Documentation.
- Interprofessional communication.

## Internationally Qualified Nurse Pathway

Create dedicated inventory covering:

- NCNZ registration pathways.

This should become a separate future SEO and learner acquisition stream.
