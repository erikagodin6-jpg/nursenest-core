# Allied Health Learning Experience Parity

All Allied Health pathways should feel like first-class NurseNest products. Parity means the same premium learner shell, navigation, progress tracking, report cards, analytics, subscription gating, accessibility, and card system used by RN, RPN, and NP pathways, while keeping education profession-specific.

## Layout Parity

Required across Respiratory Therapy, Paramedicine, Medical Laboratory Technology, Occupational Therapy, Physical Therapy, Social Work, Psychotherapy, Personal Support Worker, and future Allied Health professions:

- Theme-aware design
- Canonical navigation
- Shared learning shell
- Progress tracking
- Report cards
- Analytics
- Subscription gating
- Shared card layouts
- Accessibility standards

No Allied profession should fork a separate shell or feel like a secondary product.

## Hint System

All Allied questions should support contextual hints that guide reasoning without revealing the answer. Examples:

- Respiratory Therapy: "Consider how oxygenation differs from ventilation before choosing the priority action."
- Paramedicine: "Prioritize the most immediate life-threatening concern using scene safety and ABCs."
- MLT: "Review which laboratory value is most clinically significant and whether it requires urgent communication."
- Physical Therapy: "Consider which intervention promotes the greatest functional improvement while respecting safety limits."
- Occupational Therapy: "Focus on activities of daily living, independence, and the environment where the task occurs."

## Clinical Pearls

Clinical pearls should be short, memorable, practice-relevant, and profession-specific. They appear after completion as part of the learning surface, not as generic nursing filler.

## Premium Rationales

Every Allied question should follow this structure:

- Correct Answer
- Why It Is Correct
- Why The Other Options Are Incorrect
- Clinical Application
- Exam Strategy
- Clinical Pearl
- Related Topics

Rationales must use each profession's real-world frame: ventilation and ABGs for RT, scene and transport decisions for Paramedicine, specimen integrity and QA for MLT, functional outcomes for PT, ADLs and adaptive strategies for OT, advocacy and risk escalation for Social Work, therapeutic alliance and safety for Psychotherapy, and dignity/safety/reporting for PSW.

## Content Counts

Allied hubs should display real counts from platform data:

- Questions
- Flashcards
- Lessons
- Simulations
- Clinical Skills

Avoid vague labels such as "Included", "Available", or "Ready" where counts are available.

## Simulations And Clinical Skills

Each profession must have profession-specific activities. Examples include ventilator management for RT, trauma calls for Paramedicine, critical value reporting for MLT, mobility assessments for PT, home safety and ADL planning for OT, risk screening for Social Work, crisis intervention for Psychotherapy, and safe transfers for PSW.

## Readiness And Report Cards

Readiness domains should match the profession. Examples:

- RT: Airway Management, Ventilation, Oxygenation, ABG Interpretation
- Paramedicine: Scene Management, Trauma Care, Medical Emergencies, Cardiac Care
- MLT: Specimen Integrity, Lab Interpretation, Quality Control
- PT: Mobility, Rehabilitation, Functional Outcomes
- OT: ADLs, Cognitive Function, Adaptive Strategies

## Marketing Parity

Dedicated Allied screenshots must not reuse nursing screenshots. Each profession should have:

- Question Bank Screenshot
- Flashcard Screenshot
- Lesson Screenshot
- Simulation Screenshot
- Clinical Skills Screenshot
- Readiness Dashboard Screenshot

Screenshots should show real learning states, not hub navigation.
