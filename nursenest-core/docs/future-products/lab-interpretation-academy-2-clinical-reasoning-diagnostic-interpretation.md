## Phase 2: Core Interpretation Framework

Every lab activity must teach the learner to move from abnormal result to clinical reasoning. Range memorization is not enough.

### Required Activity Template

| Step | Required Prompt | Required Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | What is abnormal? | Lab name, patient value, reference context, severity. |
| 2 | Why is it abnormal? | Likely physiologic causes and patient-specific contributors. |
| 3 | What does it mean clinically? | Connection to assessment findings, diagnosis, complications, or treatment response. |
| 4 | What trend matters? | Direction, speed of change, baseline comparison, response to intervention. |
| 5 | What complication may occur? | Safety risk if missed or delayed. |
| 6 | What must be done next? | Scope-appropriate nursing/clinical action, reassessment, monitoring, preparation. |
| 7 | When is escalation required? | Critical value, rapid trend, symptoms, instability, treatment failure. |
| 8 | What happens if missed? | Deterioration pathway, medication harm, organ injury, delayed treatment. |

### Standardized Content Types

- Single-lab interpretation card
- Trend interpretation drill
- Multi-lab pattern case
- Critical-value response case
- Medication monitoring case
- Diagnostic reasoning puzzle
- Simulation-linked lab decision
- Escalation communication script
- Remediation flashcard set

## Phase 11: Global Reuse Strategy

Lab interpretation is highly reusable globally, but units, critical-value policies, reporting workflows, medication names, and scope expectations require overlays.

| Asset Category | Classification | Expected Reuse Rate | Supplement Needed |
|---|---|---:|---|
| Core physiology and pathophysiology | Global | 90-95% | Language/local terminology only. |
| Electrolyte interpretation | Global | 85-90% | Unit display, critical thresholds, escalation policies. |
| ABG interpretation | Global | 85-90% | Reporting units, RT/RN role overlays, escalation policies. |
| Trend interpretation | Global | 85-90% | Local reporting norms and documentation expectations. |
| Critical-value response | Country/institution-specific overlay | 65-80% | Critical-value policy, notification workflow, documentation. |
| Medication monitoring | Global with country supplement | 75-85% | Medication names, therapeutic ranges, formularies, scope. |
| Diagnostic reasoning cases | Global with role overlay | 75-85% | Role-specific depth and scope boundaries. |
| Institutional reports | Institution-specific | 50-65% | Competency language, thresholds, reporting structure. |

Reuse goal:

- Global clinical interpretation core: 85-90%.
- Country/institution overlays: only where units, policies, scope, documentation, or medication naming differs.
- Role overlays: PN/RPN, RN, NP, allied health, emergency, critical care, and MLT depth controls.
