# Editorial Policy

**Last updated:** April 2026

NurseNest publishes exam-preparation and nursing education content for learners in the United States and Canada. This policy describes how we create, label, and maintain that content so it remains accurate, useful, and trustworthy for **Your Money Your Life (YMYL)** health-education contexts.

## Purpose and scope

- **Purpose:** Support licensure and certification exam readiness with structured lessons, practice items, and explanatory articles—not to provide individualized medical advice or replace institutional protocols.
- **Scope:** Pathway lessons, blog articles, programmatic study guides, tool copy, and related marketing education surfaces.

## Editorial standards

1. **Clinical alignment** — Topics are mapped to recognized exam blueprints (e.g., NCLEX-style domains, pathway-specific competencies) and updated when blueprints or high-stakes exam emphasis materially change.
2. **Source-aware authoring** — Clinical and pharmacology content is drafted against current, widely used references and practice expectations. Where guidelines differ by jurisdiction, we call out country- or pathway-specific notes when feasible.
3. **Transparency** — Educational articles display author and review attribution when available. When a byline is not yet populated, content still follows this policy and our [Content review policy](/content-review-policy).
4. **Corrections** — Factual errors reported by users or identified in review are triaged, corrected, and logged through our content workflows.

## AI-assisted content

Some drafts may use AI tools under human oversight. AI output is **edited, fact-checked, and validated** against exam scope and clinical plausibility before publication. AI is not used as the sole reviewer for YMYL medical claims.

## Advertising and independence

Educational content is separated from promotional placement. Subscription or product mentions appear where contextually appropriate (e.g., study features) and do not dictate clinical claims.
