# Failed the NCLEX? Now What

Failing the NCLEX can feel devastating, but many successful nurses do not pass on their first attempt.

The most important step is identifying:
- weak content areas,
- clinical judgment gaps,
- and testing strategy issues.

Students recovering from a failed NCLEX attempt should focus on:
- adaptive practice exams,
- rationale review,
- SATA practice,
- and prioritization.

Strong remediation plans are usually structured around question-based learning rather than passive rereading.
