# How Many Times Can You Fail the NCLEX?

Many nursing students worry about what happens if they fail the NCLEX.

The good news is that candidates can retake the NCLEX, although rules vary by jurisdiction.

Most nursing regulatory bodies require:
- waiting periods,
- remediation,
- or registration renewal after multiple attempts.

Students who fail should focus on:
- identifying weak areas,
- improving clinical judgment,
- and increasing adaptive practice question exposure.

Failing the NCLEX does not mean you cannot become a successful nurse.
