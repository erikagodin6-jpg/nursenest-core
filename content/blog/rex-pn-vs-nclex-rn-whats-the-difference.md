# REx-PN vs NCLEX-RN: What’s the Difference?

Many Canadian nursing students wonder how the REx-PN compares to the NCLEX-RN. Although both exams use computerized adaptive testing (CAT), they evaluate different scopes of nursing practice.

Understanding the differences can help students prepare more effectively and choose the right study strategies.

## What Is the REx-PN?

The REx-PN is the Canadian practical nursing licensing examination used for registration as a Registered Practical Nurse (RPN) or Licensed Practical Nurse (LPN) depending on the province.

The exam focuses on:
- foundational nursing care,
- stable and predictable clients,
- safety,
- communication,
- and practical nursing judgment.

## What Is the NCLEX-RN?

The NCLEX-RN is the licensing examination for Registered Nurses.

Compared with the REx-PN, the NCLEX-RN evaluates:
- more complex clinical judgment,
- advanced prioritization,
- delegation,
- unstable client management,
- and broader care coordination.

## Similarities Between the Exams

Both exams:
- use computerized adaptive testing,
- adjust question difficulty dynamically,
- focus heavily on safety,
- include NGN-style clinical judgment,
- and require application rather than memorization.

## Major Differences

### Scope of Practice

The NCLEX-RN tests broader independent nursing responsibilities.

The REx-PN focuses more on practical nursing responsibilities within defined care environments.

### Clinical Complexity

NCLEX-RN questions often involve:
- unstable patients,
- rapid prioritization,
- and delegation decisions.

REx-PN questions generally focus on:
- predictable patients,
- routine care,
- and practical nursing interventions.

### Question Style

Both exams include:
- SATA questions,
- case studies,
- prioritization,
- and clinical judgment.

However, NCLEX-RN questions are typically more complex and layered.

## Which Exam Is Harder?

Neither exam is easy.

The NCLEX-RN is generally considered more clinically demanding because it evaluates RN-level responsibilities and decision-making.

The REx-PN still requires strong preparation and critical thinking.

## Best Study Strategies

Successful students usually focus on:
- adaptive practice questions,
- clinical reasoning,
- rationales,
- and repeated CAT-style exposure.

Passive studying alone is usually insufficient.

## Key Takeaway

The REx-PN and NCLEX-RN are both adaptive nursing licensure exams, but they evaluate different scopes of practice and levels of clinical complexity.

Understanding these differences helps students prepare more effectively and choose the right study resources for their professional pathway.
