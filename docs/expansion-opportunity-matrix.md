## Opportunity Notes

### Institutional Sales

Evidence:

- `InstitutionalOrganization`, `InstitutionalMembership`, `InstitutionalCohort`, and license-event models exist.
- Admin analytics, weak-area, product intelligence, and content-quality surfaces exist.

Best wedge:

- Cohort readiness dashboard.
- Weak-area heatmaps.
- Faculty-assigned remediation.
- Clinical judgment progress reports.

### Hospital Partnerships

Best wedge:

- New-grad transition and specialty readiness.
- Simulation debriefs.
- Unit-specific onboarding pathways.
- Competency passport.

Risk:

- Procurement and clinical governance requirements are much higher than learner subscriptions.

### Allied Health

Evidence:

- Allied routes and reports show multiple occupations and learner surfaces.

Best wedge:

- Start with roles where clinical readiness and exam prep overlap: MLT, respiratory therapy, pharmacy tech, paramedic, imaging.

### International Markets

Evidence:

- Global architecture docs and registries support future regions.
- Inventory for several future RN markets is not yet paid-product ready.

Best wedge:

- English-first markets with clear exam/regulator frameworks.
- Keep `/exams/{country}` discovery separate from paid product routes.

### Faculty Tools

Best wedge:

- "Which students are stuck and why?"
- "Which clinical judgment step is failing?"
- "Which content should I reteach?"
- "Which remediation assignment should I send?"

## Expansion Principles

- Expand where current platform primitives compound.
- Avoid country mini-apps or specialty mini-apps.
- Do not launch paid international products before content, pricing, reviewer, and support gates pass.
- Favor B2B wedges that reuse existing learner data rather than require a separate LMS.

## Conclusion

The best expansion sequence is: institutional readiness dashboards, new-grad/hospital transition, branching simulations, allied/NP, then international paid markets. This sequence maximizes defensibility while reducing product sprawl.
