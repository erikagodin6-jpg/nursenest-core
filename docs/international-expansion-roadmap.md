# International Expansion Roadmap

Date: 2026-05-31

Purpose: define NurseNest's international nursing expansion sequence, content-reuse strategy, localization requirements, and publication readiness gates after domestic RN, PN, and NP pathways are established.

This roadmap is content-reuse-first. NurseNest should maximize reuse of existing lessons, flashcards, questions, rationales, hints, clinical pearls, simulations, NGN-style content, ECG, labs, pharmacology foundations, and clinical reasoning assets while localizing the parts that genuinely differ by country, regulator, scope of practice, documentation standard, medication naming, terminology, or exam blueprint.

## Expansion Preconditions

International expansion should begin only after domestic RN, PN, and NP pathways are established enough to provide reliable reusable source assets.

Before any international pathway enters public launch review, NurseNest must verify:

- domestic reusable source content is publication-ready,
- blueprint and specialty depth requirements are met for the target exam,
- global clinical concepts are separated from country-specific overlays,
- regulator-specific blueprint mapping exists,
- localization review workflow exists,
- country-specific reviewer workflow exists,
- billing and support readiness are defined,
- SEO discovery pages do not overclaim paid product readiness.

## Localization Requirements

Every international pathway must include localization across six domains.

### Country-Specific Scope Of Practice

Each country must define:

- RN responsibilities,
- LPN/EN responsibilities where applicable,
- NP/APN responsibilities where applicable,
- delegation boundaries,
- escalation expectations,
- medication administration scope,
- documentation expectations,
- student/new-graduate practice limits where relevant.

### Country-Specific Legislation

Each country must localize:

- documentation requirements,
- privacy laws,
- consent requirements,
- mandatory reporting obligations,
- professional accountability standards,
- controlled-substance handling where applicable,
- workplace and public-safety obligations where applicable.

### Country-Specific Medications

Each pathway must review:

- generic names,
- brand names,
- local formulary differences,
- route and preparation differences,
- medication safety terminology,
- prescribing and administration scope,
- high-alert medication policies where relevant.

### Country-Specific Clinical Guidelines

Each pathway must localize:

- resuscitation standards,
- infection prevention standards,
- maternal-child standards,
- mental health standards,
- deterioration and escalation standards,
- documentation and handoff standards,
- regulator-specific competency language.

### Country-Specific Terminology

Local terminology must appear throughout lessons, questions, rationales, hints, clinical pearls, simulations, and learner feedback.

Examples:

| Country | Preferred terminology examples |
|---|---|
| United States | ER, physician orders |
| United Kingdom | A&E, prescriber orders |
| Australia | medication charts, observation charts |

Terminology localization cannot be limited to glossary pages. It must appear inside clinical scenarios, answer choices, rationales, charts, handoffs, and simulations.

### Country-Specific Exam Blueprints

Each pathway must map content to:

- official exam domains,
- competency standards,
- skill stations where applicable,
- item format expectations,
- domain weighting,
- readiness/report-card domains,
- remediation pathways.

## Content Reuse Rules

Reuse where appropriate:

- core pathophysiology,
- anatomy and physiology,
- disease processes,
- pharmacology principles,
- ECG interpretation foundations,
- laboratory interpretation foundations,
- clinical reasoning frameworks,
- patient safety principles,
- prioritization principles,
- generic assessment frameworks,
- reusable simulation logic where scope and terminology match.

Create or rewrite content when country-specific differences exist:

- regulator-specific exam blueprint,
- scope of practice,
- medication names or local formulary,
- documentation standards,
- privacy/consent/reporting law,
- local clinical guideline,
- local terminology,
- OSCE/station workflow,
- professional role expectation,
- culturally or system-specific patient context.

## Reuse Classification

| Content type | Reuse posture | Required localization |
|---|---|---|
| Core lessons | Reuse with overlays | Terminology, guideline references, local role scope, local exam blueprint. |
| Flashcards | Reuse with overlays | Local terminology, medication names, exam relevance, common mistakes. |
| Questions | Reuse selectively | Scenario language, scope, documentation, medications, blueprint mapping, distractor plausibility. |
| Rationales | Reuse selectively | Country-specific safety standards, escalation pathways, exam strategy, terminology. |
| Hints | Reuse selectively | Avoid hints that point to US/Canada-specific workflows unless localized. |
| Clinical pearls | Reuse selectively | Must remain bedside-relevant in the local healthcare context. |
| Simulations | Reuse logic, localize presentation | Vitals, documentation, escalation, medication systems, scope, handoff language. |
| NGN content | Reuse reasoning model, localize format | Use only when compatible with local exam style or clinical reasoning pedagogy. |
| ECG | High reuse | Localize escalation, documentation, and professional role expectations. |
| Labs | High reuse | Localize units, reference ranges, reporting conventions, and clinical guidelines. |
| Pharmacology | Medium reuse | Local formulary, brand names, prescribing/administration scope, safety warnings. |

## International Publication Readiness

No country pathway may be launched until it reaches:

| Readiness area | Required threshold |
|---|---:|
| Blueprint coverage | 95% |
| Localization coverage | 95% |
| Question coverage | 95% |
| Lesson coverage | 95% |
| Flashcard coverage | 95% |
| Clinical judgment coverage | 95% |
| Adaptive learning coverage | 95% |

If any readiness area falls below 95%, the pathway remains:

```text
DRAFT - NOT READY FOR PUBLICATION
```

Only fully localized pathways may be marked:

```text
READY FOR INTERNATIONAL PUBLICATION
```

## International Launch Gate Checklist

Before public launch, each country/exam pathway must pass:

- inventory minimum for its expansion tier,
- 95% publication readiness across all required areas,
- local blueprint mapping,
- local scope-of-practice review,
- medication and terminology localization,
- local clinical guideline review,
- rationale/hint/clinical-pearl completeness,
- CAT eligibility where CAT is advertised,
- adaptive remediation routing,
- analytics and readiness domain tagging,
- author/reviewer metadata,
- country-specific SEO metadata,
- hreflang/canonical validation,
- localized pricing and checkout readiness,
- learner support readiness.

## SEO And Product Posture

International SEO discovery pages may launch before product pathways only if they are truthful about readiness.

Allowed early:

- country nursing career guides,
- exam overview pages,
- registration pathway explainers,
- waitlist pages,
- content previews,
- free educational guides.

Not allowed before readiness gates pass:

- paid product claims,
- mature pathway claims,
- CAT readiness claims,
- full question-bank claims,
- localized simulation claims,
- exam-pass outcome claims,
- public navigation to incomplete paid pathways.

## Recommended Build Sequence

1. Convert international pathway planning into registry-driven country/profession/exam rows.
2. Add tier-specific inventory targets to pathway readiness dashboards.
3. Build reusable global clinical content overlays.
4. Build UK CBT and Australia RN localization overlays first.
5. Build New Zealand after UK/Australia inheritance is proven.
6. Build UAE exam overlays only after Gulf terminology, regulator, and medication review workflows are in place.
7. Build India and Philippines only when enough localized recruitment/licensure content exists to avoid NCLEX repackaging.
8. Keep Tier 5 markets as SEO research/discovery until Tier 1-4 are 95% publication-ready.

## Success Criteria

NurseNest expands internationally without creating disconnected mini-products or shallow country clones.

The successful model is:

```text
Global clinical foundation
-> country-specific localization overlay
-> regulator/exam blueprint overlay
-> pathway-specific learning assets
-> adaptive remediation and readiness analytics
-> market-specific SEO and conversion funnel
```

This protects educational quality, maximizes content reuse, reduces redevelopment cost, and prevents international launch claims from exceeding actual pathway readiness.
