# NurseNest Outcomes Reporting Framework

**Date:** 2026-05-31  
**Purpose:** Allow NurseNest to report learner outcomes ethically without unsupported pass-rate, readiness, or success claims.

## Core Rule

No pass-rate, exam-success, readiness-prediction, or employment/admission claim may be published unless NurseNest can show:

- Data source.
- Date range.
- Denominator.
- Numerator.
- Inclusion/exclusion criteria.
- Verification method.
- Confidence interval or uncertainty statement when statistical.
- Limitations.
- Whether data is self-reported, verified, or platform-observed.

## Claim Taxonomy

| Claim type | Example | Requirements |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Anecdotal story | "A learner reported passing NCLEX after using NurseNest." | Consent, no generalization, no implied pass rate. |
| Self-reported survey result | "Among survey respondents..." | Survey method, sample size, date range, response bias disclosure. |
| Verified outcome | "Verified pass" | Documentation or acceptable verification method, consent, privacy controls. |
| Platform usage metric | "Questions completed" | Event definition, date range, bot/test-user exclusion. |
| Readiness model claim | "Predicted readiness" | Model method, validation status, limitations, not a guarantee. |
| Comparative claim | "Better than competitor" | Independent evidence required; otherwise prohibited. |

## Prohibited Without Evidence

- "Guaranteed pass" unless legal terms, eligibility, limitations, and refund/extension policy are explicit.
- "X% pass rate" without denominator/methodology.
- "Clinically proven" without study design and source.
- "Most trusted" without credible survey methodology.
- "Official NCLEX" or regulatory endorsement unless formally authorized.
- Any claim implying NCSBN, nursing board, school, hospital, or regulator endorsement without proof.

## Success Story Collection

Collect:

- Learner name display preference.
- Profession/pathway.
- Country/state/province.
- Exam or outcome.
- Date of outcome.
- NurseNest features used.
- Study duration.
- Consent for display.
- Verification status.
- Whether story can be used in marketing, SEO, ads, or institutional materials.

## Outcome Methodology Page

Create a public page: `/outcomes-methodology`.

It should explain:

- What NurseNest tracks.
- What NurseNest does not claim.
- How self-reported outcomes are collected.
- How verified outcomes differ from testimonials.
- How pass-rate calculations are performed when used.
- How confidence intervals are calculated.
- How users can correct or remove a story.
- Privacy protections.

## Pass-Rate Reporting Standard

Pass-rate claims require:

- Minimum sample threshold.
- Clear cohort definition.
- First-time vs repeat tester split.
- Exam and country.
- Test date range.
- Product usage threshold.
- Verification level.
- Confidence interval.
- "Individual results vary" disclosure.
- No direct competitor comparison unless independently validated.

## Readiness Prediction Standard

Readiness assessments must disclose:

- Inputs used.
- Whether model is validated against real outcomes.
- Confidence band.
- Limitations.
- That readiness is educational guidance, not a guarantee.
- Recommended next actions.

## Dashboard Requirements

Track internally:

- Outcomes submitted.
- Outcomes verified.
- Outcomes rejected.
- Consent status.
- Profession.
- Exam.
- Country/region.
- Features used.
- Time-to-outcome.
- Renewal/conversion correlation.

## Compliance and Trust

- Do not expose personally identifiable details without consent.
- Do not publish school/employer/program names unless consent permits it.
- Do not imply institutional endorsement from individual student success.
- Allow story removal.
- Maintain audit logs for changes to outcome claims.

## Initial Recommendation

Until a formal methodology exists, NurseNest should use conservative language:

- "Learner success stories"
- "Self-reported outcomes"
- "Readiness indicators"
- "Educational guidance"

Avoid:

- Broad pass-rate percentages.
- Implied guarantees.
- Competitor superiority claims.
